Even when calamity strikes beyond Singapore shores, the Singapore Red Cross (SRC) can be counted on to help! The SRC is proactive in helping communities abroad prepare and build resilience against disasters even before they occur by conducting training and capacity-building activities. When a disaster strikes, SRC is quick on the scene to provide first aid, medical care, and psychosocial support. It also offers affected communities with effective post-disaster response with focus on recovery efforts through rehabilitation and rebuilding livelihoods. Recent examples of SRC’s assistance to overseas communities-in-need include a fundraiser for refugees in the Afghanistan Crisi s and distribution of oxygen tanks and ventilators amid the Covid19 pandemic in India.

SRC also established the Restoring Family Links programme to assist in reuniting families separated by natural disasters or armed conflict. It uses the tracing network of the International RCRC Movement to help bring together loved ones displaced during a crisis.

The SRC Youth runs the Overseas Humanitarian Programme (OHP) that enables Red Cross Youth cadets to engage in operations that relieve human suffering, protect human lives and dignity and respond to emergencies. In 2017, a group of secondary school students helped a school in Malaysia increase their attendance ratings.

In addition, SRC provides supplementary educational and enrichment programmes for children and youths, such as Psychological First Aid (PFA), to encourage the young to become responsible and socially conscious citizens, In World Restart A Heart Day 2021, participants learned how to use and apply basic first aid, acquire a basic knowledge of CPR and the use of AED.

SRC also encouraged more local efforts and training on World Restart A Heart Day 2021. The Red Cross opened training sessions for basic first aid during this event, allowing locals and others to learn how to use and apply basic first aid. SRC also has humanitarian education for those interested in playing a part in building society. This event further encouraged many others to acquire basic knowledge of CPR and the use of AED, allowing locals to feel a sense of belonging while contributing to the needy in society.

(COLE)

For senior citizens, SRC launched ElderAid, a befriending programme to promote resilience, security and robustness for the elderly who are living in solitud-e. Volunteers visit the elderly to carry out social and recreational wellness activities. Home Monitoring and Eldercare (HoME+) is another form of aid providing many benefits. SRC installs this HoME+ system that allows it to monitor and respond to emergencies in an elderly’s home. There is also the Community Health on Wheels(CHoW) service that enables SRC beneficiaries to be ferried to their medical appointments.

Disaster response and recovery programmes spearheaded by the SRC are therefore comprehensive and holistic. It renders assistance to vulnerable communities in strengthening their capacity to withstand natural catastrophes pre-crisis, provides critical rescue and medical assistance during emergencies, and offers post-crisis expertise to help communities return quickly to normalcy.

At the launch of the third tranche of the voucher scheme on Monday at Jurong Spring Community Club, Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong said the vouchers are to express the Government's appreciation for everyone's solidarity amid the pandemic.

They should also help to defray some daily expenses such as food and essential household items and services, as well as support local heartland shops and hawkers that have been hit hard during the pandemic, he said.

He noted that the fight against Covid-19 has been a long and winding journey and the country's response to it has had to adapt accordingly when the virus "surprised us multiple times".

He said: "It's been challenging for everyone, especially for our residents and for our heartland businesses, to keep up with the new policies and changing situations.

"Thank you for bearing with us, and for your continued trust and support as we make our way through these difficult times."

The voucher scheme has gone digital so that they are easier to use, said PM Lee, who is also chairman of the People's Association, which CDCs come under.

Merchants can better track transactions and receive payment faster with less hassle, while residents have the convenience and flexibility of digital vouchers, he added.

The earlier tranches of the voucher scheme required residents to collect physical vouchers from community centres and clubs.

Mr Sheam Kannan, 29, who lives with his parents in Bukit Batok, said that the digital vouchers make it more convenient for his family, compared with physical ones.

For example, if they were to go grocery shopping, any of them could click on the link at any point in time, rather than have physical vouchers that would be held by one person, or could even be misplaced.

Having the link to share with family members is also useful, as “it reduces the need and trouble of having to log into Singpass individually”, added Mr Sheam, a senior executive in the social service sector.

PM Lee said the CDCs have been working with government agencies and community partners over the past few months to design the scheme with a good user experience and reach out to heartland businesses to explain how it works and to get them on board.

He added that help will be available to residents at all community centres and clubs, SG Digital community hubs, as well as selected residents' committee or residents' network centres in areas with a higher proportion of the elderly.

Silver Generation Office ambassadors will also conduct house visits to explain the voucher scheme to seniors.

"We will learn from this experience so that the next time we have such a scheme, it will be even more simple and convenient for everyone," said PM Lee.

The launch was also attended by mayors of the five districts, who chair the community development council in their area.
